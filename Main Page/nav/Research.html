Facilities & Labs

From high-performance computing to electron microscopy, the University provides researchers with access to state-of-the-art facilities and equipment.

Princeton manages the Department of Energy's Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory, a national laboratory that uses plasma science to solve some of the world's toughest science and technology challenges.

The University also collaborates with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration to advance climate modeling and research at the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory.

  • Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory (PPPL)
  • Geophysical Fluid Dynamics Laboratory (GFDL)
  • High-Performance Computing Research Center

Nobel Laureate David MacMillan Joins Princeton's Fight Against Cancer

MacMillan has been named a Distinguished Scholar in the Princeton Branch of the Ludwig Institute for Cancer Research, which leverages the power of collaboration across scientific fields to advance cancer research.

Scientists Map the Half-Billion Connections That Allow Mice to See

Princeton SPIA's Program on Science and Global Security Celebrates 50 Years

Princeton Precision Health

An interdisciplinary, AI-driven approach to tackling big questions about health and disease — bringing together faculty from engineering, medicine, genomics, and public policy.

Mapping an Entire Fly Brain

Princeton researchers have produced a complete connectome of the Drosophila brain — a landmark step toward understanding diseases of the human brain at the neural level.

Rewriting Neanderthal Narratives

Princeton geneticists are challenging long-held assumptions about ancient humans, drawing on genomic data to reconstruct migration, interbreeding, and cultural exchange across millennia.

Collaboration Is Key

It is through collaborations that Princeton's faculty transform laboratory research into real-world projects that can make a difference to society. Princeton researchers collaborate across academic fields and across the globe, with the campus home to numerous interdisciplinary research centers. External partnerships with industry, government, and international institutions extend Princeton's reach even further.

Research Resources

Administration

Office of the Dean for Research

Services include Research and Project Administration, Technology Licensing and New Ventures, Strategic Partnerships and Engagement, Research Integrity and Assurance, and Laboratory Animal Programs. The Dean's office is the central hub for compliance, funding, and research support across all divisions.

Entrepreneurship

Office of Innovation

Princeton's Office of Innovation is a key resource for faculty, researchers and entrepreneurs who want to build a collaborative ecosystem to spur real progress on society's most pressing challenges. It manages technology licensing, startup formation, and strategic industry partnerships.

Interdisciplinary

Research Centers & Institutes

Princeton is home to dozens of interdisciplinary research centers and institutes, including the Andlinger Center for Energy and the Environment, the Princeton Institute for International and Regional Studies (PIIRS), the Center for the Study of Democratic Politics, and the Lewis-Sigler Institute for Integrative Genomics.

External Partnerships

Princeton faculty members collaborate with colleagues throughout the world to carry out important, cutting-edge research and scholarship. These partnerships span government agencies, industry leaders, international universities, and non-governmental organizations.

  • Princeton Innovation — industry and startup ecosystem
  • Strategic Partnerships — government and NGO collaboration
  • Technology Licensing — bringing discoveries to market
  • International Research — global field work and partnerships
