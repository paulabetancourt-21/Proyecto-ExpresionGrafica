Student Life

Most Princeton students live on campus, and they eat, study, serve, work, play, pray and relax here too. The University has many programs in place to support your interests and help you feel at home.

The vibrant intellectual and social life within each residential college includes a network of undergraduate and graduate students, faculty fellows and staff who share meals, events, and everyday life together.

Athletics

Varsity

Varsity Teams

Princeton has one of the largest and most successful athletic programs in NCAA Division I and the Ivy League. About 20 percent of undergraduates participate in varsity sports, spanning 38 teams across all major sports.

Tickets

Princeton Athletic Ticket Office

Attend a sporting event and cheer on the Tigers to victory. Student tickets are available at reduced rates and many regular-season home games offer free admission with a valid Princeton ID.

Recreation

Campus Recreation

Campus Recreation offers a range of fitness and wellness opportunities including intramural sports, club sports, instructional programming, and a fully equipped fitness center open to all students.

Serving the Public Good

Service is infused in the lives of Princetonians. Students engage in service activities beginning with Orientation. Faculty partner with nonprofit organizations through their research, employees volunteer with local organizations, and alumni often dedicate their lives to service. The Pace Center for Civic Engagement coordinates community-based learning, civic engagement for graduate students, and faculty and staff outreach programs.

Cultural Life & Families

Identity

Cultural & Affinity Groups

Students explore their own cultures and learn about peers' cultures through cultural and affinity-based student centers and groups. More than 50 identity-based organizations are active on campus each year.

Faith

Religious Life

Many faith communities thrive on campus. Individuals can practice and explore their faith, get spiritual counseling, and participate in interfaith activities such as meditation and civic engagement year-round.

Families

Families at Princeton

When a student enrolls at Princeton, their family becomes part of our community too. Families can take part in campus activities, Freshman Families Weekend, and alumni programs across the globe. A dedicated Families Handbook provides guidance throughout the year.

Living in Princeton, N.J.

Tree-lined streets, specialty shops, restaurants, parks and a friendly and safe atmosphere all make Princeton a wonderful place to spend time in.

Located midway between New York City and Philadelphia, Princeton offers the best of small-town charm with easy access to major metropolitan areas via train.

  • Princeton town center — shops, cafés, restaurants
  • NJ Transit rail to NYC & Philadelphia
  • Nassau Street — the University's main corridor
  • Carnegie Lake and natural parks nearby
