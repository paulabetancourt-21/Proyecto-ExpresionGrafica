Studying at Princeton

Princeton is one of the top universities in the world. Our undergraduate and graduate programs emphasize learning, creativity, innovation and collaboration.

We are a community of learning enriched by the wide range of experiences and perspectives of our students, faculty and staff. Undergraduate students benefit from the extraordinary resources of a world-class research institution dedicated to undergraduate teaching, while our graduate students engage in original and independent scholarship and research.

Library

The Princeton University Library system houses more than 10 million books, along with extensive digital holdings, in buildings across campus.

Undergraduates, graduate students and faculty have access to a remarkable array of resources for their course work, independent research and scholarship.

  • Firestone Library — Main research collection
  • Mudd Manuscript Library — Rare & archival materials
  • Lewis Library — Sciences collection

Academic Calendar

Dates & Deadlines

Academic Calendar

Princeton's academic year is organized into two semesters. The fall semester runs from early September through January, and the spring semester from February through May. Important registration, drop/add, and examination deadlines are published by the Registrar's Office at the start of each term.

Exams

Exam Schedules

Final examinations are held during dedicated exam periods at the end of each semester. The Dean of the College coordinates the scheduling to minimize conflicts across departments and programs.

Tools

Course Tools

Princeton uses Canvas as its primary learning management system. Faculty post syllabi, assignments, and course materials there. Additional academic tools include undergraduate announcement boards and graduate academic portals maintained by the Graduate School.

Advising & Tutoring

We provide students with a range of academic resources and a support network of faculty and staff to support their success. The McGraw Center for Teaching and Learning offers tutoring, study groups, writing support, and academic skills workshops throughout the year.

Learning Abroad

Princeton is a global institution, and there are vast opportunities to learn and pursue research beyond the University's campus. Geographically extensive programs offer Princeton students many ways to connect with the world.

  • Office of International Programs
  • Bridge Year — gap year service abroad
  • PIIRS Global Seminars

Beyond the Classroom

Career

Career Development

The Center for Career Development empowers students to discover their interests and pursue their passions. Services include one-on-one guidance, recruiting events, internship resources, and alumni mentorship programs.

Lifelong Learning

Continuing Education

The University sponsors programs that allow alumni, community members and students from other universities to experience Princeton's unique educational offerings, including the Community Auditing Program.

Innovation

Innovative Learning

Learning at Princeton goes beyond the traditional classroom. Technology enables creative and innovative educational opportunities across campus and around the world, supported by the McGraw Center for Teaching and Learning.
